Biological & Linguistic Anthropology

Understanding Human Biology and Language

Biological anthropology focuses on human evolution, genetics, and primates, while linguistic anthropology studies the history and structure of language.

Subfields of Biological & Linguistic Anthropology

Subfield Focus Example Study Notable Anthropologist
Paleoanthropology Fossil evidence of human ancestors Study of Lucy (Australopithecus afarensis) Donald Johanson
Primatology Behavior and evolution of primates Chimpanzee communication Jane Goodall
Phonetics Sounds and pronunciation in languages Differences between English and Mandarin tones Peter Ladefoged
Sociolinguistics Language and society Regional dialects in the U.S. William Labov

Why These Fields Matter

Both subfields help us understand human evolution and cultural development. They provide insights into how humans adapt biologically and communicate across cultures.
